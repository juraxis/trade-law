# CIT Decision Brief Template — Trade-Lawyer Assessment

## Structure

Generate CIT/CAFC decision assessments with this structure. Follow Bluebook citation conventions from `reference/formatting-standards.md`. This template produces a **trade-lawyer-quality** assessment, not merely a case summary.

---

### Header Block

```
CIT/CAFC DECISION BRIEF — DRAFT WORK PRODUCT

Date:           {current date}
Case:           {full case caption}
Court:          {CIT / CAFC}
Decision Date:  {date of decision}
Docket/Slip Op: {docket number and/or slip opinion number}
Judge:          {presiding judge (CIT) or panel (CAFC)}
Prepared by:    AI Classification Research Assistant
Status:         DRAFT — For Attorney/Broker Review
```

---

### 1. Case Overview

**One-paragraph summary** of the case: who sued, what the dispute was about, and how the court resolved it. Written for a busy trade lawyer who needs to quickly assess relevance.

---

### 2. Procedural History

- **CBP classification:** {HTS subheading CBP assigned}
- **Importer's proposed classification:** {HTS subheading importer argued}
- **Protest:** {date filed, basis}
- **Prior proceedings:** {any prior CIT or administrative proceedings}
- **Basis of jurisdiction:** 28 U.S.C. § {1581 subsection}
- **Type of proceeding:** {trial / summary judgment / motion to dismiss}

---

### 3. Facts

**Product description:** {detailed description of the imported merchandise as found by the court}

**Key facts relied on by the court:**
- {Fact 1 — with citation to opinion}
- {Fact 2}
- {Fact 3}

**Disputed facts (if any):** {facts the parties disagreed on and how the court resolved them}

---

### 4. Issues Presented

1. {Primary legal question — e.g., "Whether the product is properly classified under heading 8471 as an ADP input unit or under heading 8543 as an electrical machine"}
2. {Secondary issues, if any}

- {Key precedent cases the court relied on}

> "{The key sentence or passage from the opinion that a trade lawyer would cite — the dispositive language}"

### 7. Holding

- **Classification determined:** {HTS subheading}
- **Prevailing party:** {Importer / Government}
- **Scope:** {Narrow (specific product only) / Broad (product category)}
- **Subsequent history:** {Appealed to CAFC? / CAFC affirmed/reversed? / Cert. denied?}

---

### 8. Impact Assessment

#### A. Effect on Classification Practice
- {How this decision affects classification of similar products}
- {Whether it narrows or expands the scope of the heading}
- {Whether it establishes a new test or refines an existing one}

#### B. CROSS Ruling Alignment
- **Rulings reinforced:** {CROSS rulings consistent with this decision}
- **Rulings undermined:** {CROSS rulings that may be inconsistent}
- **Expected CBP response:** {Will CBP issue new rulings? Modify existing ones?}

#### C. Affected Product Categories
- {What types of products are affected beyond the specific product at issue}
- {Any related or analogous products that should be re-evaluated}

---

### 9. Strategic Implications

#### A. Client Advisory
- {What should importers of similar goods do now?}
- {Should existing entries be reviewed? Protests filed? Prior disclosures made?}
- {Is reclassification at a lower rate now available?}
- {Are there retroactive implications (28 U.S.C. § 1581(a) allows 180-day protest window)?}

#### B. Litigation Outlook
- {Is appeal likely? Is appeal pending?}
- {Odds of reversal on appeal — based on standard of review, clarity of reasoning}
- {Is this consistent with other CIT judges' approach, or an outlier?}

#### C. Compliance Recommendations
- {Specific actions recommended for compliance programs}
- {Binding ruling request advisable?}
- {Monitoring recommendations for related pending cases}
